## Design Context

### Users

University CS students in Monterrey (UANL FCFM primarily), young founders building their first startups, and working developers who want to level up by building real systems. They visit the site in a state of ambition — they want to prove themselves, find serious collaborators, and get into a community that feels exclusive and high-signal. Browsing late at night after coding sessions, on their laptop in dark rooms.

### Brand Personality

**Aggressive. Elegant. Uncompromising.**

BuildersMTY is a builder collective, not a student club. The brand voice is direct, confident, and slightly intimidating — like walking into a room where everyone is clearly better than you, but they respect you for showing up. The interface should make visitors feel "I want in" and "I might not be good enough" simultaneously.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Tone**: Aggressive elegance — the precision of a luxury brand with the rawness of a hacker collective. Think a Swiss design studio that programs in C.
- **Theme**: Dark mode. These users browse at night, in dim rooms, after long coding sessions. Dark is correct for the context.
- **Reference**: impeccable.style — clean, confident, no wasted space, typography-driven
- **Anti-reference**: The current site's overuse of uppercase tracking, repetitive card grids, and "military terminal" cosplay. Also: generic SaaS landing pages, Y Combinator clones, typical dev community sites with gradient cards
- **Color**: Strong red as the singular accent. Not coral, not orange — a committed, bold red. Neutrals tinted toward the red hue for cohesion.
- **Typography direction**: Sharp, confident. Not monospace-as-lazy-tech-signal. A distinctive display face paired with a clean body face.
- **Zero border-radius stays** — this is intentional, not lazy. Sharp corners = sharp minds.

### Design Principles

1. **Scarcity creates desire** — Show less, imply more. The site should feel like you're glimpsing 10% of something much larger.
2. **Typography is the interface** — When the type is good enough, you don't need decoration. Let the words do the work.
3. **Earned complexity** — Start stark, reveal depth through interaction. Don't front-load everything.
4. **Red is rare** — The accent color appears sparingly. When it shows up, it means something.
5. **No filler** — Every element on the page must earn its space. If removing it changes nothing, remove it.
